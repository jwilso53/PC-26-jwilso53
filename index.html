    Drug Overdose Crisis

    Drug overdose deaths in the U.S. have surged dramatically in recent years, with over 100,000 fatalities reported annually, driven primarily by synthetic opioids like fentanyl. The epidemic disproportionately impacts certain communities, including rural areas and low-income populations, exacerbating existing health disparities. Public health efforts continue to focus on expanding access to treatment, harm reduction strategies, and curbing the illicit drug supply to address this crisis.

    With increasing population in most major countries and cities, there has been a rise in the use of Fentanyl. Fentanyl, a powerful synthetic opioid, is now the leading cause of overdose deaths in the U.S., accounting for over 70% of opioid-related fatalities. Its potency—up to 50 times stronger than heroin—makes even tiny amounts lethal, and it is often mixed with other drugs, increasing the risk of accidental overdoses.
